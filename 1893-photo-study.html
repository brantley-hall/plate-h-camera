Sight Line Analysis

This 1893 photograph provides a valuable record of the NYU University Building's western facade as seen from Washington Square. The camera position appears to be at ground level near the center of the square, capturing the building's full height and architectural details.

Camera Position Estimate

Based on the perspective and building proportions, the camera was likely positioned approximately 200-300 feet west of the NYU building, near the center of Washington Square. The ground-level positioning suggests the photographer was using a tripod at street level.

Building Elements Visible

The photograph clearly shows the building's five-story facade, the central tower, and the Gothic architectural elements including the pointed arches and decorative stonework. The perspective suggests a slightly elevated viewpoint relative to the building's base.

Historical Context

By 1893, the NYU University Building had been standing for nearly 60 years and was a prominent landmark in Greenwich Village. This photograph captures the building during its later years before its eventual demolition in the 1890s.

Technical Analysis

The photograph shows excellent depth of field and sharp architectural details, characteristic of large format photography of the period. The even lighting suggests either overcast conditions or careful timing to avoid harsh shadows.
